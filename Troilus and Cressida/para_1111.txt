ACHILLES :  The dragon wing of night o'erspreads the earth, And, stickler-like, the armies separates. My half-supp'd sword, that frankly would have fed, Pleased with this dainty bait, thus goes to bed. Sheathes his sword Come, tie his body to my horse's tail; Along the field I will the Trojan trail.
