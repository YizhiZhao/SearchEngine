DUKE VINCENTIO :  There was a friar told me of this man. Sirrah, thou art said to have a stubborn soul. That apprehends no further than this world, And squarest thy life according. Thou'rt condemn'd: But, for those earthly faults, I quit them all; And pray thee take this mercy to provide For better times to come. Friar, advise him; I leave him to your hand. What muffled fellow's that?  Provost  This is another prisoner that I saved. Who should have died when Claudio lost his head; As like almost to Claudio as himself. Unmuffles CLAUDIO
