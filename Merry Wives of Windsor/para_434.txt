SIR HUGH EVANS :  'Pless my soul, how full of chollors I am, and trempling of mind! I shall be glad if he have deceived me. How melancholies I am! I will knog his urinals about his knave's costard when I have good opportunities for the ork. 'Pless my soul! Sings To shallow rivers, to whose falls Melodious birds sings madrigals; There will we make our peds of roses, And a thousand fragrant posies. To shallow-- Mercy on me! I have a great dispositions to cry. Sings Melodious birds sing madrigals-- When as I sat in Pabylon-- And a thousand vagram posies. To shallow  &amp; c. Re-enter SIMPLE
